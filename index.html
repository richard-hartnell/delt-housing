THE DISSOLVING EQUITY LAND TRUST

A proof of concept.

The dissolving-equity land trust (DELT) is a cooperative housing model that creates more affordable rental housing.

It works by paying tenants back for the equity that they pay for with their rent money.

By using this model, public and private entities could reduce rental housing costs by as much as one-half of "fair" market rate.

How does it work?

Normally, landlords make a downpayment.
Then, tenants pay off the mortgage.

In this system, tenants still pay off the mortgage
but future tenants pay them back.

Then, even later tenants pay back those tenants
but the payback rate slightly decreases.

By the time distant-future tenants aren't getting paid back, the mortgage has dissolved and rent is so affordable it doesn't matter.
